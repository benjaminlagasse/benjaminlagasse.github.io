About Me

Hello! My name is Benjamin Lagasse. I am a student in my Senior year at the University of Massachusetts Amherst. I study English with a concentration in Professional and Technical Writing under the Professional Writing and Technical Communication program (PWTC).

Through my studies at UMass, I have completed technical writing projects including a MadCap Flare accessibility manual, a Microsoft Word software documentation manual, and a mock grant proposal.

Most recently, I had the opportunity to intern as a Technical Writer at Brooks Automation in Chelmsford, MA. As part of the Global Communications team I used MadCap Flare to write and update technical procedures and service manuals for Brooks Automation products in the semiconductor industry.

Outside of work I am an avid Boston sports fan and I also enjoy reading & gaming in my free time.

Technical Background

Before I started studying technical writing, I worked seasonally as an HVAC/R technician. I assisted senior technicians in completing installations and calibrations of various residential and commercial equipment across Central Massachusetts and the Greater Boston area.

During my time in the field I gained relevant training in referencing wiring diagrams and other technical literature. I used this training to become a more capable problem solver in my collaborations with senior technicians to troubleshoot system issues.

As a technical writer I aim to translate my hands-on technical experience into crafting comprehensive and accessible documentation that will improve the user experience of my audience.
